How does big data STEAL your privacy

Written by

Chris LIU

May 1, 2022 · 12 min read

How does big data steal your privacy?!

You have no privacy!

The widespread use of digital technology has created the conditions for the flow and application of data elements, which causing users’ personal information opened in the digital space. Have you encountered any of these situations in your daily life?


If you want to use any of apps, you can only choose to agree and continue, or you can only quit.


Your ladybro said that her new lipstick was beautiful, then the shopping software pushed it to you.


Once you shared out a Taobao link, other shopping apps recommended the same model to you on the homepage immediately.

It seems that big data is monitoring you silently, and you have no privacy. While you enjoy the conveniences of digital technology, you are also the victim of it. Personal privacy has become a hot topic in the whole society.




Your phone is being bugged?!

Privacy for convenience - You gave it by yourself!

So, have you ever suspected that we are being bugged by our phone? However, these software do not need to watch you all in real time. When you grant it to read your address book, network environment, precise positioning, apps only need to combine the basic information of your age and gender, consumption records and living address in your account, then they can easily paint your individual consumer picture, and accurately market the targeted goods to you.


And that's just the information that will ask for your authorization.


You can’t escape anyway – You have no choice.

Most of the time, your privacy is already played out when you are completely unaware. Many apps have features that are granted by default, for example, you may think “connect to Wi-Fi” is not harmful, but in fact this can scan the devices connected to your home Wi-Fi, infer how many people in your home, how many people are at home now, and your general purchasing power. What’s even more frightening is that since reading the sticky board is a basic function given to each application by the system, then if you just copied your bank card or ID number, it can be read when you enter any software.




Dirty industrial chain

Baidu CEO Robin LI spoke at the China High Level Development Forum, “Our people are able to treat privacy for convenience, for safety, for efficiency. In a lot of cases, they are willing to do that.” To be honest, he is right, you always authorize app location information in exchange for accurate weather, taxi, and delivery services. But when the app, Flashlight, also ask for your location access, have you ever wondered why applications are scrambling for these data that they don't even need?


The benefits behind are lucrative. On the official website of Baidu Marketing, they have been summarized as the eight advantages that attract capitalists to invest including your browsing history, installed software records, precise location and phone model, as well as your interests and fan attributes.




How to combat?

Individual level

You have to start with your own devices and turn off the dangerous settings.

Society level

· It is important to use the regulations to increase the deterrent effect and make data users responsible for data security. The problematic websites and apps should be removed.

· should improve the level of technology used in supervision, using digital technology to counter digital technology.

· Strengthen the cooperation between government and enterprises, and gather social resources to collaborate in combating violations in the digital age.


Build a better world

Digital technology is designed to make people’s lives easier, not to add hassle. We cannot deny the convenience especially in pandemic. However, digital privacy leakage impinges on our private life. The original purpose of technologies is to find users’ needs easily to adjust the supply and improve market efficiency, rather than to provide ulterior motives of the enterprise against the user’s weapon.
